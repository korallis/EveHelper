# Overview
EveHelper is an intelligent desktop companion application for EVE Online that revolutionizes ship fitting for new and intermediate players. The core problem it solves is the overwhelming complexity of ship fitting in EVE Online - with thousands of modules, complex attribute interactions, and skill dependencies, new players often struggle to create effective fittings or understand what they can realistically fly with their current skills. EveHelper analyzes a player's completed skills and automatically generates 5 optimized ship fittings ranging from maximum DPS to maximum tank, then allows customization based on specific activities (PvP objectives, mining operations, hauling cargo, or combat scenarios). This removes the guesswork and provides a clear learning path for players to understand fitting principles while ensuring they can actually use the recommended setups. The value lies in transforming EVE's steep learning curve into an accessible, guided experience that helps players make informed decisions and progress confidently in their virtual careers.

# Core Features

## 1. Intelligent Ship Browser & Search System
-   **What it does:** Provides comprehensive ship browsing and search capabilities allowing users to find ships by name, group, category, market group, or attribute filters (speed, cargo capacity, slot layout, etc.). Displays detailed ship information including base stats, slot configurations, and skill requirements.
-   **Why it's important:** With hundreds of ships in EVE Online, players need efficient ways to discover ships that match their interests, skills, and intended activities. This prevents overwhelming choice paralysis common among new players.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements advanced filtering system with multiple criteria (ship class, role, faction, attributes).
    -   Shows skill requirements with clear indicators of what the player can currently fly.
    -   Displays ship comparison tools to evaluate multiple hulls side-by-side.
    -   Integrates with market data to show estimated ship costs and availability.
    -   Provides ship role explanations and recommended use cases for each hull.

## 2. Advanced Module Discovery & Compatibility System
-   **What it does:** Enables users to discover and explore all compatible modules for any ship and slot type (High/Mid/Low/Rig/Subsystem). Provides detailed module attribute breakdowns, compatibility checks, and fitting validation in real-time.
-   **Why it's important:** Understanding which modules can fit where and how they interact is fundamental to effective ship fitting. New players often struggle with module compatibility and don't understand the vast array of available options.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Displays modules organized by slot type with intelligent filtering and search.
    -   Shows real-time compatibility checking including CPU/Power requirements and skill prerequisites.
    -   Provides detailed attribute breakdowns and effect explanations for each module.
    -   Highlights module synergies and warns about potential conflicts or suboptimal choices.
    -   Integrates market pricing and availability data for informed module selection.

## 3. Comprehensive Fitting Management System
-   **What it does:** Allows users to create, save, delete, import, and export ship fittings in standard EVE Online formats (EFT, COPY format, and custom JSON). Provides fitting organization with folders, tags, and search capabilities for efficient management of large fitting collections.
-   **Why it's important:** Players accumulate many fittings over time and need efficient ways to organize, share, and manage them. Standard format support ensures compatibility with existing EVE Online tools and community resources.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements full CRUD operations for fitting management with intuitive UI.
    -   Supports import/export in EFT format, COPY format, and application-specific JSON.
    -   Provides fitting organization with folders, favorites, and tagging systems.
    -   Includes fitting sharing capabilities and integration with popular fitting repositories.
    -   Maintains fitting history and version control for tracking changes over time.

## 4. Real-Time Fitting Statistics Engine
-   **What it does:** Calculates and displays comprehensive fitting statistics including power grid usage, CPU consumption, capacitor stability, DPS (with drones), resistances, speed, agility, and cost analysis. All calculations update in real-time as modules are added, removed, or modified.
-   **Why it's important:** Understanding fitting performance metrics is crucial for effective ship building. Real-time feedback helps players learn cause-and-effect relationships between module choices and ship performance.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements accurate EVE Online calculation algorithms including stacking penalties.
    -   Accounts for character skills, implants, and ship bonuses in all calculations.
    -   Provides visual indicators for resource usage (CPU/Power) with warning systems.
    -   Calculates effective DPS including drone contributions and optimal range considerations.
    -   Shows detailed resistance profiles and effective HP calculations for survivability analysis.

## 5. Intelligent Multi-Objective Fitting Generator
-   **What it does:** Automatically generates five optimized ship fittings tailored to specific goals: Max DPS (maximum damage output with drones and implants), Max Tank (prioritizing survivability), Well-Rounded (balanced approach), and two intermediate variants. Includes mission-specific optimization based on enemy NPC damage types.
-   **Why it's important:** New players struggle to understand fitting trade-offs and often create suboptimal builds. Automated generation with clear objectives helps players understand different approaches while ensuring they get effective, usable fittings.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Uses multi-objective optimization algorithms to balance competing goals (DPS, tank, utility).
    -   Factors in player skills, available modules, and resource constraints for realistic builds.
    -   Generates mission-specific fittings by analyzing enemy damage types and resistance requirements.
    -   Provides clear explanations for each fitting's design philosophy and intended use case.
    -   Automatically adjusts recommendations as player skills improve or new modules become available.

## 6. Advanced Skill Planning & Optimization
-   **What it does:** Creates custom skill training plans optimized for flying desired ships efficiently. Analyzes skill gaps for specific fittings and generates time-optimized training sequences that maximize benefit per training day invested.
-   **Why it's important:** Skill training in EVE Online requires long-term planning, and poor choices can waste months of training time. Optimized plans help players reach their goals efficiently while understanding the progression path.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Analyzes target ships and fittings to identify required and beneficial skills.
    -   Calculates optimal training order considering skill prerequisites and training time.
    -   Provides multiple plan options (minimum requirements vs. optimal performance).
    -   Shows training time estimates and milestone achievements throughout the plan.
    -   Integrates with ESI to compare plans against current skill queue and suggest adjustments.

## 7. Live Market Data Integration
-   **What it does:** Retrieves and displays real-time market prices for ships and modules alongside fitting recommendations. Shows wallet information, transaction history, and provides cost analysis for complete fittings across different market hubs.
-   **Why it's important:** Cost is a significant factor in fitting decisions, especially for new players with limited ISK. Current pricing helps players make informed decisions and budget effectively for their ship builds.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Fetches current market data via ESI API with intelligent caching to respect rate limits.
    -   Displays prices from major trade hubs with comparison tools.
    -   Shows fitting total costs with individual module pricing breakdowns.
    -   Integrates with player wallet data to show affordability indicators.
    -   Provides price history and trend analysis for expensive items.

## 8. Visual Fitting Interface & Compatibility Alerts
-   **What it does:** Provides an intuitive WPF-based visual interface for fitting ships with drag-and-drop module placement, real-time compatibility checking, and visual alerts for fitting issues (CPU/Power overload, skill requirements, etc.).
-   **Why it's important:** Visual feedback makes the fitting process more intuitive and helps players understand ship layouts and module relationships. Immediate error feedback prevents frustration and helps players learn fitting constraints.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements interactive ship layout visualization with slot representations.
    -   Provides drag-and-drop module placement with immediate validation feedback.
    -   Shows visual indicators for resource usage, skill requirements, and compatibility issues.
    -   Includes contextual help and tooltips explaining any errors or warnings.
    -   Supports multiple viewing modes (detailed stats, simplified layout, comparison view).

## 9. Comprehensive Data Caching & Offline Capability
-   **What it does:** Downloads and maintains local caches of EVE Online Static Data Export (SDE), item images, and market data for offline functionality. Provides automatic cache management with intelligent update mechanisms.
-   **Why it's important:** Reliable offline functionality ensures the application remains useful even without internet connectivity. Efficient caching improves performance and reduces load on EVE Online's APIs.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Downloads and caches complete SDE data for ships, modules, skills, and game mechanics.
    -   Implements efficient image caching for ship renders, module icons, and corporation logos.
    -   Provides cache management tools with manual refresh and automatic update scheduling.
    -   Maintains data integrity checks and fallback mechanisms for corrupted cache data.
    -   Optimizes storage usage while ensuring comprehensive offline functionality.

## 10. Secure EVE Online Integration
-   **What it does:** Authenticates via EVE Online SSO to securely retrieve character skill lists, asset inventories, wallet information, and skill queue data. Maintains secure token management with automatic refresh capabilities.
-   **Why it's important:** Integration with live character data ensures recommendations are always relevant to the player's current capabilities. Secure authentication protects player data while providing seamless integration.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements OAuth 2.0 PKCE flow for secure character authentication.
    -   Retrieves character data via ESI API including skills, assets, wallet, and skill queue.
    -   Maintains secure token storage with automatic refresh and error handling.
    -   Provides multi-character support for players with multiple accounts.
    -   Respects ESI rate limits and caching headers for optimal API usage.

## 11. Persistent User Data & Preferences
-   **What it does:** Saves and persists user preferences, favorite fittings, custom skill plans, and application settings between sessions using local storage. Provides backup and restore capabilities for user data protection.
-   **Why it's important:** Users invest significant time in creating fittings and skill plans. Reliable persistence ensures this investment is protected and easily accessible across application sessions.
-   **How it works at a high level:**
    -   Implements robust local storage using LiteDB for structured data persistence.
    -   Provides automatic backup mechanisms with configurable retention policies.
    -   Supports data export/import for backup and migration purposes.
    -   Maintains user preferences including UI layout, default settings, and customizations.
    -   Includes data integrity checks and recovery mechanisms for corrupted data.

# User Experience

## User Personas
1.  **The Overwhelmed Newcomer (0-6 months playing):**
    -   **Needs:** Simple, effective fittings they can actually use; clear explanations of why fittings work; guidance on skill training priorities.
    -   **Pain Points:** Information overload from existing tools; fittings they can't use; losing ships due to poor fits; not understanding game mechanics.
    -   **Goals:** Get into the game quickly with working setups; learn gradually without being overwhelmed; avoid expensive mistakes.

2.  **The Progressing Player (6-18 months playing):**
    -   **Needs:** Optimization within skill constraints; activity-specific fitting advice; clear upgrade paths as skills improve.
    -   **Pain Points:** Knowing they could do better but not sure how; understanding trade-offs between different approaches; efficiently planning skill training.
    -   **Goals:** Optimize their current capabilities; understand fitting principles; plan character progression effectively.

3.  **The Returning Player (Taking a break and coming back):**
    -   **Needs:** Quick catch-up on what they can currently fly; updated fittings for current game meta; refresher on fitting principles.
    -   **Pain Points:** Game changes while away; old fittings may be outdated; forgetting what they were working toward.
    -   **Goals:** Get back into the game quickly; understand current capabilities; adapt to any game changes.

## Key User Flows
1.  **Getting Ship Recommendations:**
    -   User selects a ship they're interested in (browse by category, search, or favorites).
    -   App analyzes their current skills and displays 5 optimized fittings with clear statistics.
    -   User selects their intended activity from guided categories (PvP type, PvE enemies, mining, hauling).
    -   App refines recommendations based on activity and explains the reasoning.
    -   User can save preferred fittings, export to game, or explore upgrade paths.

2.  **Learning and Skill Planning:**
    -   User views a recommended fitting and clicks "How can I improve this?"
    -   App shows skill gaps and their impact on fitting effectiveness.
    -   User selects skills they're interested in training.
    -   App generates optimized training plan showing time investment and improvements.
    -   User can add recommended skills to their in-game training queue.

3.  **Activity-Specific Guidance:**
    -   User selects "I want to mine" or "I want to do PvP" from the main interface.
    -   App guides them through specific choices (ore types, PvP scenarios, etc.).
    -   Shows ship recommendations suitable for their skills and chosen activity.
    -   Provides activity-specific tips and warnings for each recommended fitting.

## UI/UX Considerations
-   **Simplicity First:** Clean, uncluttered interface that doesn't overwhelm new players with too much information at once.
-   **Progressive Disclosure:** Basic information visible immediately, with detailed explanations available on demand.
-   **Visual Learning:** Charts, graphs, and visual comparisons to help players understand trade-offs and improvements.
-   **Guided Workflows:** Step-by-step processes for complex decisions, with clear explanations at each step.
-   **Contextual Help:** Integrated explanations of EVE mechanics without requiring external documentation.
-   **Responsive Feedback:** Immediate visual feedback when making changes or selections.
-   **Accessibility:** Suitable for players of all experience levels, with advanced features available but not prominent.

# Technical Architecture

## System Components
-   **EveHelper.App (WPF UI Layer):**
    -   Interactive desktop interface for viewing ships, fittings, and skill plans.
    -   Views (XAML) with MaterialDesign styling for modern, clean interface.
    -   ViewModels implementing MVVM pattern for data binding and user interaction.
    -   Navigation services for seamless flow between different app sections.
    -   User interface components optimized for fitting display, comparison, and management.
    -   Drag-and-drop fitting interface with real-time validation feedback.

-   **EveHelper.Services (Business Logic Layer):**
    -   **SDE Data Service**: Downloads and caches EVE static data, exposes types, groups, categories, and attributes from the Static Data Export.
    -   **ESI Client**: Wraps HTTP calls to EVE's ESI API for character info, skill lists, market prices, fittings, wallet data, and asset information.
    -   **Cache Service**: Generic in-memory and disk cache system for SDE data, images, API responses, and user data with intelligent refresh mechanisms.
    -   **Ship Data Service**: Provides comprehensive ship lists, module compatibility checking, slot counts, and detailed ship information with filtering capabilities.
    -   **Fitting Service**: Create, save, delete, import, export fittings in multiple formats (EFT, COPY, JSON) and calculate comprehensive fitting statistics including DPS, tank, capacitor, and resource usage.
    -   **Image Service**: Downloads and caches item icons, ship images, corporation logos, and character portraits as byte arrays with efficient storage management.
    -   **Skill Service**: Builds and optimizes skill plans based on piloting goals, analyzes skill gaps, and generates time-optimized training sequences.
    -   **Market Service**: Retrieves and caches real-time market prices, transaction history, and wallet information across multiple trade hubs.
    -   **Module Service**: Provides module discovery, compatibility analysis, and detailed attribute breakdowns for all slot types.

-   **EveHelper.Core (Models & Interfaces Layer):**
    -   Core data models for ships, modules, fittings, skills, player data, and market information.
    -   Service interfaces ensuring separation of concerns and testability.
    -   Business logic models for optimization algorithms, calculations, and data transformations.
    -   EVE Online specific models for SDE types, groups, categories, and attributes.
    -   ESI response models for character data, market data, and API integration.

-   **EveHelper.Data (Data Access Layer):**
    -   SDE data access and parsing logic for ships, modules, skills, and game mechanics.
    -   Local cache management with LiteDB for structured data persistence and offline functionality.
    -   Data transformation services for converting between ESI responses and internal models.
    -   Static asset and image caching with automatic update mechanisms.
    -   User data persistence for fittings, skill plans, preferences, and application state.

## Data Models (Key Examples)
-   `OptimizedFitting`: Represents a generated fitting with effectiveness scores and explanations.
-   `SkillAnalysis`: Contains player's current skills with gaps and training recommendations.
-   `ActivityProfile`: Defines optimization parameters for specific activities (PvP, mining, etc.).
-   `FittingComparison`: Structure for side-by-side fitting analysis and statistics.
-   `TrainingPlan`: Optimized skill training sequence with time estimates and benefits.
-   `ShipCapability`: Analysis of what a player can effectively do with a specific ship and their skills.

# Risks and Mitigations

## Technical Challenges

**Complex Optimization Algorithms:**
-   **Risk:** Generating truly optimal fittings requires sophisticated algorithms that balance multiple objectives while respecting numerous constraints.
-   **Mitigation:** Start with simpler heuristic approaches and iteratively improve. Research existing fitting optimization techniques. Focus on "good enough" recommendations initially, then enhance accuracy over time.

**EVE Online Game Complexity:**
-   **Risk:** EVE's fitting mechanics involve complex interactions, stacking penalties, skill bonuses, and edge cases that could lead to incorrect recommendations.
-   **Mitigation:** Extensive testing against known good fittings. Implement comprehensive unit tests for calculation engines. Study existing tools and community knowledge. Start with simpler ships and gradually handle more complex cases.

**Performance with Large Datasets:**
-   **Risk:** Optimization algorithms may be computationally intensive, especially when considering thousands of possible module combinations.
-   **Mitigation:** Implement intelligent pruning of search space. Use caching for repeated calculations. Consider background processing for complex optimizations. Profile performance regularly and optimize bottlenecks.

## Figuring out the MVP that we can build upon

**Defining "Good Enough" Recommendations:**
-   **Risk:** Setting the bar too high for initial recommendations could delay delivery, while setting it too low could provide little value to users.
-   **Mitigation:** Focus on basic PvE fittings first (simpler optimization targets). Get user feedback early and iterate. Implement simple optimization before complex multi-objective algorithms.

**Scope Management:**
-   **Risk:** EVE Online has many ship classes, activities, and edge cases that could lead to scope creep and indefinite development.
-   **Mitigation:** Start with a subset of popular ships and common activities. Define clear success criteria for each phase. Resist feature creep until core functionality is solid.

## Resource Constraints

**Algorithm Development Expertise:**
-   **Risk:** Optimization algorithms require specialized knowledge that may not be immediately available to the development team.
-   **Mitigation:** Start with simple rule-based approaches. Research academic papers and existing implementations. Consider consulting with EVE Online community experts. Build incrementally from basic to advanced algorithms.

**Game Knowledge Requirements:**
-   **Risk:** Effective fitting recommendations require deep understanding of EVE Online mechanics and meta.
-   **Mitigation:** Engage with the EVE Online community early for feedback. Study existing tools and their approaches. Test recommendations against known effective fittings. Implement feedback mechanisms for user corrections.

**Testing and Validation:**
-   **Risk:** Validating that recommendations are actually good requires extensive testing across many scenarios and ship types.
-   **Mitigation:** Implement automated testing against known benchmarks. Create test cases for common scenarios. Plan for community beta testing. Build metrics to measure recommendation effectiveness.
